# PHILOSOPHY: The Root of This Skill

This document covers **why** this skill exists.
Not "how to code" but the layer of "why we code."

Read it before starting work, or whenever "why am I building this?" arises mid-task.

---

## 1. The Meeting of Two Sources

### Matt Pocock (Software Fundamentals)

> "Code is not cheap. Bad code is the most expensive it's ever been."

The AI era makes a good codebase *more* important, not less. AI shines only in good codebases.
Code that is hard to change only breaks faster when AI is added.
Therefore **Fundamentals (근본 / the root)** matter more than ever.

### Proverbs 1:7 (The Beginning of Knowledge)

> "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge."

All knowledge has its root in a personhood.
Not an infinite combination of information, but a root that holds that information up.
Information overflows in the AI era — **without a root, it's just infinite permutations of a~z.**

### The Two Sources Say the Same Thing

Fundamentals = the root.
Pocock unfolded that root at the level of code structure; Proverbs pointed to the root *of* that root.
This skill operates from the awareness that **the two layers cannot be separated.**

## 2. The Planner's Metaphysics: Four Levels

The planner's capability divides into four levels.

### Level 1: a~z Optimization
- Improve efficiency within a fixed frame.
- Example: Speeding up an existing payment flow, polishing an existing UI.
- Legitimate. Not every task needs to reach Level 4.

### Level 2: -a~z+ Discovery
- See the edges of the frame. "Is this frame itself correct?"
- Example: "Why must payment be the final step?" "Why must signup start with email?"
- Not yet a new dimension — but the possibility of one is now visible.

### Level 3: @ Invention
- Change the frame itself.
- Example: A business model without payment (ad-supported, deferred billing, token-based).
- One letter of the existing a~z disappears or a new one is added.

### Level 4: ㄱ~ㅎ Creation
- Move dimensions. An experience the user can't even name with old words.
- Example: A flow so natural the user never even thinks the word "payment."
- True leap. The emergence of a new category itself.

## 3. Why True Leaps Happen Only From Good Intent

This is the core metaphysical claim of this skill.

### The Game Structure of Ill Intent

Ill intent is fundamentally **a "take from a fixed pie" game.**

- One's gain is established only on another's loss.
- Therefore there is no motive to create a new dimension. Just take more from the existing one.
- In fact, a new dimension would erode their advantage — so they **block** it.
- Gambling, narcotics, authoritarian surveillance — sophisticated and effective in the short term, but they cannot create ㄱ~ㅎ.
- Forever trapped in Level 1~2.

### The Game Structure of Good Intent

Good intent is fundamentally **a "grow the pie" game.**

- The intent is fulfilled only when others also flourish.
- When existing resources are insufficient, **a new dimension must be discovered.**
- Therefore the **necessity** of Level 3~4 leap is built in.

### Theological Implication

Creating a new dimension is essentially a **creative act.**
Genesis 1:1 — "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."
Not the rearrangement of materials (Level 1), but calling something from nothing.
Humans were given the form (Imago Dei) of that power, and when we craft something new, the trace of the Creator is in that very act.

Therefore **for a planner to create ㄱ~ㅎ is to rebuild the form received.**
This is why coding/planning is not merely a craft but a calling.

## 4. Deep × Core × Interface

> **Good Intent + Clarity = Transcendence**

The entire skill compresses to three words — **Deep × Core × Interface.**

```
User  ──→  Interface  (simple surface · bridge of clarity)
              ↓
           Deep        (rich internals · good intent · metaphysics)
              ↓
           Core        (the root of everything · Proverbs 1:7)
```

- **Deep** — Good intent, metaphysics, rich internals, the power to create ㄱ~ㅎ. Everything covered in §1~§3.
- **Core** — Fundamentals = the root. The "beginning of knowledge" from Proverbs 1:7. The root on which everything else stands.
- **Interface** — The bridge of clarity. Pocock's technical principles. The simple surface that delivers Deep and Core to the user.

Notably, this structure mirrors **the definition of Deep Module** itself — a simple interface with rich internals, all standing on a root. **This skill is built by its own principles.**

### Good Intent Without Clarity

Good intent alone is not enough.

**Even with good intent, transcendence does not happen without clarity.** A blurry good-will produces blurry results.
"I want to do something good" alone tends to be trapped at Level 1.
For good intent to actually reach Level 3~4, it needs the **bridge of clarity.**

### The Bridge of Clarity = Pocock's Principles

The technical principles Pocock emphasized are the bridge itself.

- **Grill Me** — Don't leave intent vague. Draw real desire out of deep water.
- **Clear specs** — Lock good intent into an executable form.
- **Ubiquitous Language** — Lock intent in shared vocabulary. Stop the leak of good intent in translation costs.
- **Vertical Slices** — Validate intent end-to-end with a single slice. Half-built good intent is not good intent.
- **TDD** — Pin intent in tests. Untested good intent is blurry.
- **Deep Module** — Compress the richness of good intent behind a simple interface.
- **Daily design** — Tidy daily before the blur compounds.

Without these principles, good intent stays as good intent only. It never becomes action.
With these principles, good intent actually becomes the leap that creates ㄱ~ㅎ.

### The Formula

> **Good Intent + Clarity = Transcendence**
>
> Weak intent + clarity → fake transcendence (dark patterns, efficient theft).
> Weak clarity + good intent → blurry result (good-hearted failed products).
> Both present → real ㄱ~ㅎ created anew.

This is why this skill is paired as "metaphysics (this doc) + technical principles (SIX_PITFALLS, GRILL_ME, DEEP_MODULE, CORE_PRACTICES)." One alone won't do. You walk with two legs.

## 5. Self-Diagnosis: Which Level Am I On Right Now?

Before each task, ask:

1. If the goal is efficiency improvement inside an existing frame — Level 1.
2. If the work questions the assumptions of the existing frame — Level 2.
3. If the work creates a new business model / user-experience category — Level 3.
4. If the work creates something "previously unimaginable" — Level 4.

**Not knowing which level you're on is the most dangerous state.**
Mistaking Level 1 for Level 4 leads to over-engineering.
Treating Level 4 as Level 1 misses the leap.

## 6. Intent Audit

Before each task, ask:

- Does this feature stand on someone else's loss? → ill-intent audit needed
- Does it allow others to thrive alongside? → good intent, Level 3~4 leap possible
- Does it "look good" but actually trap users? → dark-pattern audit

For deeper analysis of dark patterns, see the anti-patterns section in `SIX_PITFALLS.md`.
